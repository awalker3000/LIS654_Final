Institutional Considerations

Determine the Purpose and Goals of the Project

In the earliest planning stages, organizations will need to outline their goals for web archiving and understand how it aligns with the broader mission of the institution. For organizations seeking to preserve art historical resources, web archiving can combat link rot and save assets at risk from disappearing from the web and help libraries collect born-digital publications.

Many of the institutions profiled in the projects page view web archiving as an extension of their collection development policy. Some places may also be required to archive institutional websites and born digital publications in the same way they archive traditional public outreach materials. Increasingly, organizations are also choosing to capture their social media sites.

Examine Your Resources

Examining the funding and staff resources at an institution’s disposal to determine what their web archiving program will look like. It will be helpful to ask the following questions:

  • Do you already have institutional support for a web archiving program or will you need to advocate for its creation?
  • Do you have a budget line that can be leveraged for the program or will you need to find funding elsewhere?
  • Do you have in-house experts who can set up crawlers and partition server space or will you need to find a tool, like Archive-It that someone can use without in-depth technical knowledge?
  • Which type of staff will be involved in the web archiving program: archivists, librarians, digital projects staff, information technology staff? Will students contribute to the program?
  • Which staff members will curate your collections? Will they also be responsible for quality control?

Decide Who Will Have Access & How is it Granted

Many institutions want to provide open access to all their collections, but some collections may need to be private for a time. Other institutions may want to restrict use of the web archives to certain IP addresses.

If a collection is accessible, an organization will need to determine how it will promote the archived sites and how they will specifically provide access to the public. The technology and interface used to do this will need to be chosen. Archive-It users are able to view archived sites though the Wayback Machine.

Some users of Archive-It build branded web pages to introduce the web archiving program and link to the Archive-It page.

Adopt a Preservation Policy

In order to best preserve the collections captured by their web archiving programs, institutions should ask themselves the following questions:

  • How will you store and preserve your WARC files and related metadata?
  • Will you store data in your own repository or will you let a service like Archive-It handle storage?
  • Will you have multiple copies of your data in multiple locations for redundancy?

Manage Risk

Institutions will have to determine how they will manage any potential risks related to copyright. Some organizations choose to seek written permission from every website owner before they begin capture while others chose to take advantage of “fair use” granted to libraries and archives to use copyrighted materials for purposes such as teaching and research. Each web archiving program should also determine whether they will respect robots.txt or whether they will ignore this script in order to fully capture a site.

In cases where permission is not obtained before harvesting, having a clearly stated removal policy is also important.


Day-to-Day Considerations

Curation

At most institutions, only a handful of staff will choose which websites to included in each collection. Specific seeds, or URLs, will instruct crawlers what to harvest. The purpose and goals of the web archiving program will help to determine what is collected. Some organizations only capture their own web pages and social media, while others also collect assets that compliment their print collections.

It is important to be aware of and collaborate with other institutions to avoid duplicating resources.
